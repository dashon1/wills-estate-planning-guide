# Wills & Estate Planning Guide — Protect Your Family's Future

A comprehensive estate planning system that walks you through creating a legally valid will, understanding your options, and protecting everything you've built for the people who matter most.

---

## What It Is

The Wills & Estate Planning Guide is a complete digital download system covering every aspect of estate planning — from writing a basic will to healthcare directives, trust fundamentals, business owner considerations, and state-specific requirements. It's built for people who know they need a plan but don't know where to start, and for those who already have outdated documents that need reviewing.

Most people put off estate planning because it feels complicated, expensive, or morbid. This guide removes all three barriers: it's clear, affordable, and focused on what matters — making sure your family isn't left with legal chaos when they're already dealing with loss.

---

## Who It's For

- Adults with any assets, property, or dependents who don't have an up-to-date will
- Parents who need to designate guardians for minor children
- Small business owners who need business continuity planning
- Anyone with an outdated will (more than 3 years old or post major life event)
- People who want to avoid probate and simplify asset transfer

## How to Use

### First-Time Users
1. Read the complete Wills & Estate Planning Guide first
2. Fill out the Assets Inventory Template to catalog what you have
3. Use the Legal Requirements Checklist as your working checklist
4. Review the Glossary for any unclear terms
5. Read the Executor Duties Guide — share it with whoever you designate as executor

### Updating an Existing Will
1. Review the Legal Requirements Checklist to identify what needs updating
2. Update your Assets Inventory with current information
3. Premium users: cross-reference your state's specific requirements

### Premium Users
1. Complete all Starter documents first
2. Review the State-Specific Guide for your state's requirements
3. Complete healthcare directive and POA documents
4. Business owners: review Business Owner Estate Planning before finalizing

## Quick Start Checklist

Before starting:
- [ ] Gather identification documents (license, passport)
- [ ] List all financial accounts and approximate values
- [ ] Identify all real estate holdings
- [ ] Review beneficiary designations on 401k, IRA, insurance
- [ ] Decide who will serve as executor
- [ ] Name a guardian for any minor children
- [ ] Locate any existing wills or trust documents
