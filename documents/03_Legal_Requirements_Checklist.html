Pre-Planning Requirements

Complete before drafting your will

Identity Verification

Valid Government-Issued ID Obtain a valid driver's license, passport, or state ID. This confirms your identity and age for witnesses.
Driver's license number documented
Passport number documented (if applicable)
Full Legal Name Confirmation Verify your full legal name as it appears on official documents. Use this exact name in your will.
Social Security Number Ready Have your SSN available for estate planning documents. Do not include it in the will itself.

Mental Capacity Requirements

Understanding of Document Nature Confirm you understand that you are creating a will - a legal document that controls asset distribution after death.
Knowledge of Assets Have a general understanding of the nature and extent of your property and assets.
Recognition of Heirs Understand who your natural heirs are - family members who would typically inherit from you.
Mental Capacity Note

If there is any question about your mental capacity (due to illness, medication, or other factors), consult with a medical professional before executing your will.

Age Requirements

Minimum Age Verification Confirm you are at least 18 years old (or legally married, or have been emancipated by court order in some states).
Legal Requirements Checklist 2

Document Preparation

Steps for preparing your will document

Document Format

Written Document Your will must be in writing. Oral wills are generally not recognized in any U.S. state.
Typed or Handwritten Either a typed document or a fully handwritten (holographic) will is acceptable. Typed is preferred for clarity and validity.
Clear Language Use unambiguous language. Avoid vague terms like "my belongings" - specify items or categories.

Required Content Elements

Opening Declaration Include standard opening: "I, [full legal name], being of sound mind, declare this to be my Last Will and Testament."
Full legal name appears in opening
Sound mind declaration included
Revocation of Prior Wills Include language revoking any previous wills: "I revoke all previous wills and codicils."
Beneficiary Designations Clearly name each beneficiary with full legal names. Include relationship if not obvious.
Primary beneficiaries named
Contingent beneficiaries named
Executor Appointment Name your executor with full legal name. Include alternate executor in case first choice is unavailable.
Guardian Appointment (if applicable) If you have minor children, name both primary and alternate guardians.
Signature Block Include a clearly marked signature line at the end of your will, before witness attestation.
Witness Attestation Block Include formal attestation language for witnesses to sign, confirming they observed your signature.
Legal Requirements Checklist 3

Witness Requirements

Critical execution requirements for valid signing

Witness Eligibility

Minimum Age Both witnesses must be at least 18 years old (or 21 in some states). Verify age before the signing.
Mental Competence Witnesses must be mentally competent - able to understand what they are witnessing and testify if needed.
Presence During Signing Both witnesses must be present when you sign the will. They must either see you sign or acknowledge your signature.

Witness Number Requirements

Two-Witness Standard Most states require two witnesses. Verify your state's specific requirements before execution.
State Type Witness Requirement Standard States 2 witnesses required Holographic Will States May require 0-2 witnesses depending on circumstances Self-Proving Affidavit States 2 witnesses + notarization

Witness Information Required

Full Legal Name Each witness must provide their full legal name as it appears on their ID.
Current Address Include current street address (not just city) for each witness. This may be needed for court verification.
Signature Each witness must sign the will, confirming they witnessed your signature and believe you were of sound mind.
Important

Ideally, witnesses should not be beneficiaries in your will to avoid potential conflicts. If witnesses are beneficiaries, the gift to them may still be valid but could be subject to greater scrutiny.

Signing Procedure

Step-by-step execution process

Pre-Signing Checklist

Document Review Complete Final review of will content. All sections complete, no blank spaces that could be filled later.
Witnesses Available Two eligible witnesses are present and prepared to sign. Their IDs are verified.
Location Prepared Signing takes place in a quiet, private location. Adequate space for all parties.
Notary Available (if using self-proving affidavit) If your state supports self-proving affidavits, a notary is present with their seal.

Signing Sequence

Step 1: Testator Signs You sign the will in the presence of both witnesses. Use the same signature as on your ID.
Sign in permanent ink (blue or black)

Step 2: Witnesses Sign Immediately after your signature, both witnesses sign the will, attesting they witnessed your signing.

Step 3: Self-Proving Affidavit (if applicable) If using, complete and notarize the self-proving affidavit immediately while all parties are present.
Witness Declaration

Legal Requirements Checklist 5

Storage and Filing

Proper handling after execution

Document Storage

Safe Location Selected Choose a secure location for your original will: home safe, bank safe deposit box, or with your attorney.
Original Document Secured Store the original will (not copies). Keep it in a fireproof, waterproof container.
Multiple Copies Avoided Keep one official copy if needed. Multiple originals can cause confusion about which is valid.

Executor Notification

Executor Informed Your executor knows the location of your will and has access instructions.
Location Documented Written instructions with will location given to executor, alternate executor, and a trusted person.
Attorney Copy (if applicable) If your attorney prepared the will, they have a copy on file as backup.

Important Records

Death Certificate Location Known Instructions for obtaining death certificates are documented for your executor.
Asset Inventory Available Complete asset inventory is stored with or accessible to your executor.
Professional Contacts Listed Attorney, accountant, and financial advisor contact information is accessible.
Bank Safe Deposit Box Note

If storing your will in a safe deposit box, check state law. Some states prohibit wills in bank boxes due to access restrictions upon death. Consider a home safe or attorney safe as alternatives.

Legal Requirements Checklist 6

State-Specific Requirements

Verify requirements for your state
State Category Requirements Community Property States AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI - Spouse may have rights to half of community property regardless of will provisions. Holographic Will States ~25 states recognize handwritten wills without witnesses. Verify your state. Self-Proving Affidavit States Most states offer notarized self-proving affidavit to speed probate. Check availability. Inheritance Tax States IA, KY, MD, NE, NJ, PA - Additional planning may be needed for inheritance tax.

State Verification Steps

Determine Your State Confirm the state where you are domiciled (permanently reside). This determines which laws apply.
Research State Requirements Verify witness requirements, notarization options, and any special provisions for your state.
Consult State Bar or Attorney For complex situations or property in multiple states, consult a licensed estate planning attorney in your state.
